Kerala Chicken Biriyani

A fragrant rice dish cooked with chicken, aromatic spices, and herbs. Kerala-style biriyani uses Kaima or Jeerakasala rice and is known for its distinct preparation method and flavor profile. This specialty from Kasaragod is cooked in layers with meat and rice, resulting in a flavorful and aromatic dish.

Prep Time

30 minutes

Cook Time

60 minutes

Total Time

90 minutes

Servings

6 servings

(132 reviews)

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tablespoons Kashmiri red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon garam masala
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt to taste

For the Rice:

  • 3 cups Kaima rice or Jeerakasala rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
  • 6 cups water
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4 green cardamoms
  • 4 cloves
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 1 star anise
  • Salt to taste

For the Masala:

  • 1/4 cup ghee
  • 3 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 4-5 green chilies, slit
  • 2 sprigs curry leaves
  • 1/2 cup mint leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup coriander leaves, chopped

For garnishing:

  • 1/4 cup fried onions
  • 2 tablespoons cashews, fried
  • 2 tablespoons raisins, fried
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

Marinating the Chicken

  1. 1

    In a large bowl, combine the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, Kashmiri red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt.

  2. 2

    Mix well, ensuring all the chicken pieces are coated with the marinade. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight for the best flavor.

Preparing the Rice

  1. 1

    Wash the rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Drain well.

  2. 2

    In a large pot, bring 6 cups of water to a boil. Add bay leaves, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, star anise, and salt.

  3. 3

    Add the drained rice and cook until it's 70% done (about 5-6 minutes). The rice should be slightly firm when pressed between your fingers.

  4. 4

    Drain the rice and set aside. Save some of the rice water for later use.

Cooking the Chicken

  1. 1

    Heat ghee in a large, heavy-bottomed pot or biryani handi.

  2. 2

    Add the sliced onions and fry until they turn golden brown. Remove half of the fried onions and set aside for garnishing.

  3. 3

    Add green chilies and curry leaves to the remaining onions in the pot. Saut√© for a minute.

  4. 4

    Add the marinated chicken pieces and cook on medium heat for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  5. 5

    Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil starts to separate from the masala.

  6. 6

    Add half of the mint and coriander leaves. Mix well and cook for another 5 minutes until the chicken is about 80% cooked.

Layering and Dum Cooking

  1. 1

    Reduce the heat to low. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken masala.

  2. 2

    Sprinkle the remaining mint and coriander leaves over the rice.

  3. 3

    Add about 1/4 cup of the reserved rice water around the edges of the pot. This will create steam and help in the dum cooking process.

  4. 4

    Top with the reserved fried onions, cashews, and raisins.

  5. 5

    Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. Traditionally, the lid is sealed with dough to prevent steam from escaping, but you can also use aluminum foil, pressing it tightly around the edges of the pot.

  6. 6

    Place the pot on very low heat (use a heat diffuser if available) and cook for 25-30 minutes. This slow cooking on dum (steam) is what gives the biryani its distinctive flavor and aroma.

  7. 7

    Turn off the heat and let it rest for 10 minutes before opening the lid.

Serving

Gently mix the layers, bringing the chicken from the bottom up into the rice. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with raita, pickle, and pappadam.

Tip: In Kerala, biriyani is traditionally served on a banana leaf, which imparts a subtle aroma and flavor to the dish. If available, you can warm a banana leaf and serve the biriyani on it for an authentic experience.

Chef's Notes

"Kerala biriyani, especially from Kasaragod, has its own unique character compared to other regional variants. We use shorter-grained Kaima rice instead of Basmati, which gives a different texture and absorbs flavors differently."

"The key to an exceptional biriyani lies in the quality of your spices. I recommend using freshly ground spices whenever possible, and don't rush the 'dum' cooking process - that slow steaming is what makes all the difference in melding the flavors together."

Fara, Kasaragod
