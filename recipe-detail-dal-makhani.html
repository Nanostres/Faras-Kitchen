Dal Makhani

A rich and creamy lentil dish made with whole black lentils (urad dal) and kidney beans (rajma), slow-cooked with spices and finished with butter and cream. This iconic Punjabi dish is known for its deep, complex flavors developed through long, slow cooking.

Prep Time

8 hours (soaking)

Cook Time

3 hours

Total Time

11 hours

Servings

6 servings

(112 reviews)

Ingredients

For the Dal:

  • 1 cup whole black lentils (sabut urad dal)
  • 1/4 cup red kidney beans (rajma)
  • 4-5 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the Tempering:

  • 3 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 2 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste

For the Finishing:

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)

Instructions

Soaking and Cooking the Lentils

  1. 1

    Wash the black lentils and kidney beans thoroughly. Soak them together in plenty of water for at least 8 hours or overnight.

  2. 2

    Drain the soaked lentils and beans. Place them in a pressure cooker with 4-5 cups of fresh water and 1 teaspoon of salt.

  3. 3

    Pressure cook for about 15-18 whistles or until the lentils and beans are completely soft and mushy. If using a regular pot, cook for about 1.5-2 hours, adding more water if needed.

  4. 4

    Once cooked, mash some of the lentils with the back of a ladle to create a creamy texture. Keep aside.

Preparing the Tempering

  1. 1

    Heat ghee in a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven.

  2. 2

    Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.

  3. 3

    Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown, about 5-7 minutes.

  4. 4

    Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for another 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.

  5. 5

    Add the tomato puree, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and coriander powder. Cook until the oil starts to separate from the masala, about 5-7 minutes.

Combining and Slow Cooking

  1. 1

    Add the cooked lentils and beans to the tempering. Mix well.

  2. 2

    Add salt to taste and about 1 cup of water if the dal is too thick. The consistency should be like a thick soup.

  3. 3

    Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for at least 1 hour, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. The longer you cook, the richer the flavor will be.

  4. 4

    If the dal becomes too thick during cooking, add a little hot water to adjust the consistency.

Finishing Touches

  1. 1

    After the slow cooking is complete, add the garam masala and crushed kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves). Mix well.

  2. 2

    Add the butter and cream. Stir gently to incorporate.

  3. 3

    Simmer for another 5-10 minutes on low heat.

  4. 4

    Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and a swirl of cream before serving.

Serving

Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice. Dal Makhani tastes even better the next day as the flavors develop further.

Tip: For an authentic restaurant-style flavor, you can add a small piece of charcoal to a metal bowl placed in the center of the dal. Pour a little ghee on the charcoal, and when it starts to smoke, quickly cover the pot with a lid. Let it sit for 5 minutes to infuse a smoky flavor into the dal.

Chef's Notes

"Dal Makhani is all about patience - the longer you cook it, the better it tastes. In traditional Punjabi homes, it's often cooked overnight on a low flame, allowing the flavors to develop and intensify."

"The key to a rich and creamy dal makhani is to mash some of the lentils during cooking to release their starch. This creates that signature creamy texture without having to add too much cream."

Fara, Kasaragod
