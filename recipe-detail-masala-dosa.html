Masala Dosa

A crispy, golden-brown fermented crepe made from rice and lentil batter, filled with a spiced potato filling. This iconic South Indian dish is a staple breakfast in Kerala and throughout South India, known for its delicate crispness and flavorful filling.

Prep Time

12 hours

Cook Time

30 minutes

Total Time

12 hours 30 minutes

Servings

4 servings (8 dosas)

(124 reviews)

Ingredients

For Dosa Batter:

  • 2 cups dosa rice (or parboiled rice)
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 2 tablespoons chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Water as needed

For Potato Masala:

  • 4 medium potatoes, boiled and peeled
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 inch piece ginger, finely chopped
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped cilantro

For Coconut Chutney:

  • 1 cup grated fresh coconut
  • 2-3 green chilies
  • 1 inch piece ginger
  • 2 tablespoons roasted chana dal
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • For tempering: 1 tsp oil, 1/2 tsp mustard seeds, 1 dried red chili, curry leaves

Instructions

Preparing the Dosa Batter

  1. 1

    Wash the dosa rice, urad dal, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds separately. Soak them together in enough water to cover by about 2 inches for 4-6 hours.

  2. 2

    Drain the water and grind the soaked ingredients to a smooth batter, adding water as needed. The consistency should be similar to pancake batter - not too thick or too thin.

  3. 3

    Transfer the batter to a large container (it will rise during fermentation), add salt, and mix well. Cover and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight.

  4. 4

    After fermentation, the batter should have risen and have a slightly sour aroma. Stir the batter gently before making dosas.

Making the Potato Masala

  1. 1

    Roughly mash the boiled potatoes, keeping some texture. Set aside.

  2. 2

    Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add cumin seeds, curry leaves, green chilies, and ginger. Sauté for a minute.

  3. 3

    Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent. Add turmeric powder and mix well.

  4. 4

    Add the mashed potatoes and salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.

  5. 5

    Garnish with chopped cilantro and turn off the heat. Let the potato masala cool slightly before using as filling.

Preparing Coconut Chutney

  1. 1

    In a blender, combine grated coconut, green chilies, ginger, roasted chana dal, and salt. Add a little water and grind to a smooth paste.

  2. 2

    Transfer to a serving bowl.

  3. 3

    For tempering, heat oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add dried red chili and curry leaves. Fry for a few seconds until fragrant.

  4. 4

    Pour this tempering over the coconut chutney and mix well.

Making Masala Dosa

  1. 1

    Heat a non-stick or cast iron dosa tawa (flat pan) on medium heat. Sprinkle a few drops of water to check if the tawa is hot enough - the water should sizzle immediately.

  2. 2

    Pour a ladleful of batter in the center of the tawa. Using the back of the ladle, spread the batter in a circular motion to form a thin crepe. Start from the center and work outwards to form a large, thin circle.

  3. 3

    Drizzle a teaspoon of oil around the edges and over the dosa. Cook until the bottom turns golden brown and crisp, and the top appears dry.

  4. 4

    Place a portion of the potato masala on one half of the dosa. Using a spatula, gently fold the dosa over the filling to form a semi-circle.

  5. 5

    Transfer to a serving plate. Repeat with the remaining batter and filling.

Serving

Serve hot masala dosa with coconut chutney and sambar. In Kerala, it's also common to serve it with a spicy tomato-based chutney.

Tip: For extra crispy dosas, spread the batter very thin and let it cook a bit longer. You can also add a teaspoon of rice flour to the batter just before making the dosas to enhance crispness.

Chef's Notes

"The key to a perfect masala dosa lies in the fermentation and the cooking technique. In Kerala, we traditionally use a specific type of rice called 'rosematta' which gives a distinct flavor and texture to the dosa. If you can find it, I highly recommend trying it!"

"I like to add a small amount of chana dal to my dosa batter as it enhances the flavor and adds a subtle nuttiness. It's a small addition that makes a big difference in the authentic taste."

Fara, Kasaragod
