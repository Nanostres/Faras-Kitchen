Fish Mulakittathu

A spicy fish curry made with kokum, tamarind, and red chilies. This traditional Kerala fish preparation is tangy and spicy, with a distinctive red color from the chilies. The name "Mulakittathu" literally means "placed with chili" in Malayalam, highlighting the central role of red chilies in this fiery dish.

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

25 minutes

Total Time

40 minutes

Servings

4 servings

(87 reviews)

Ingredients

For the Fish:

  • 750g fish (preferably king fish, mackerel, or sardines), cleaned and cut into pieces
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the Curry:

  • 3 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 2 sprigs curry leaves
  • 15-20 small shallots (pearl onions), sliced
  • 10-12 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 inch piece ginger, crushed
  • 15-20 dried red chilies
  • 1 tablespoon Kashmiri red chili powder (for color)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 4-5 pieces kudampuli (Malabar tamarind) or 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water

Instructions

Preparing the Fish

  1. 1

    Clean the fish thoroughly and cut into medium-sized pieces.

  2. 2

    Marinate the fish pieces with turmeric powder and salt. Set aside for 15 minutes.

Preparing the Curry

  1. 1

    If using kudampuli (Malabar tamarind), soak it in 1/2 cup warm water for 15 minutes. If using tamarind paste, mix it with 1/2 cup warm water and set aside.

  2. 2

    Heat coconut oil in a clay pot or heavy-bottomed pan (traditional earthenware is preferred for authentic flavor).

  3. 3

    Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Then add fenugreek seeds and one sprig of curry leaves.

  4. 4

    Add the sliced shallots, crushed garlic, and ginger. Sauté until the shallots turn translucent.

  5. 5

    Add the dried red chilies, Kashmiri red chili powder, turmeric powder, and coriander powder. Sauté for 2-3 minutes on low heat, being careful not to burn the spices.

  6. 6

    Add the kudampuli or tamarind water along with 1.5 cups of water. Bring to a boil.

  7. 7

    Add salt to taste and let the curry simmer for 5 minutes.

  8. 8

    Gently slide the marinated fish pieces into the curry. Do not stir with a spoon as it might break the fish. Instead, gently shake the pot to ensure the fish is immersed in the curry.

  9. 9

    Cover and cook on medium-low heat for 10-12 minutes until the fish is cooked through.

  10. 10

    Once the fish is cooked, add the remaining curry leaves. Turn off the heat and let it rest for 5 minutes before serving.

Serving

Serve hot with steamed rice, tapioca (kappa), or Kerala rice pancakes (appam). This curry tastes even better the next day as the flavors develop further.

Tip: In Kerala, this dish is traditionally served in the same clay pot it's cooked in, which helps maintain its temperature and enhances the earthy flavors. If you have a clay serving dish, consider using it for an authentic presentation.

Chef's Notes

"Fish Mulakittathu is a dish that showcases the bold flavors of Kerala's coastal cuisine. The key to an authentic version is using kudampuli (Malabar tamarind), which gives the curry its distinctive tangy flavor that balances the heat from the chilies."

"In Kasaragod, we traditionally use karimeen (pearl spot fish) or ayala (mackerel) for this curry, but any firm white fish works well. The earthen pot is not just for tradition - it actually enhances the flavor by slow cooking and retaining the heat."

Fara, Kasaragod

This recipe took me right back to my grandmother's kitchen in Kochi! The balance of spice and tanginess is perfect. I reduced the number of dried red chilies to 10 instead of 15-20 as we prefer it a bit milder, and it was still wonderfully flavorful. The tip about not stirring the fish but gently shaking the pot was very helpful - my fish pieces stayed intact. I couldn't find kudampuli so I used regular tamarind, and while I'm sure it's not exactly the same, the curry was still delicious!
